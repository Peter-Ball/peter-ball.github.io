This mutating welcome sign visualizes a word graph connected by close Levenshtein distances.

The Levenshtein distance is a measure of difference between two strings. It records the number of single-character edits (insertions, deletions, and substitutions) needed to transform one string into another.

I created a graph of around 23,000 words connected by Levenshtein distances of 2 or less. Every time you mouse over the text, it updates randomly to one of its neighbors on the word graph.

The visualization shows how much the information content of a string can be altered with minimal edits. Also, the word list I used to create the graph has many obscure words, making it a good resource for your next game of Scrabble!
